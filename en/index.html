It may seem trivial, but adding an extra "hello" before your typical salutatory hello allows for hello-synchronous communication. If the other party is away, and you leave before they come back, they'll be able to respond to your greetings in just a friendly a way as you greeted them.

If you feel it's a bit brusque to simply say "hello", you can still preface your message with as many more pleasantries as you see fit.

For example:

  • "hi! hello!"
  • "hello hello!"
  • "hello hello? hello hello hello hello, hello hello! üéµüé∂üéµ"
  • etc.

People who do this are generally trying to be to-the-point by jumping right into the request, but are ignoring the ways in which conversations naturally happen in person or on the phone - and that's great for productivity and efficiency! But it's 2022 and chat is neither of those things.

Your coworker is stuck at home with their cat and in desperate need of social interaction. So despite best intentions, you're actually just making the other person feel more disconnected by not wishing them hello, not just once but over and over again!
